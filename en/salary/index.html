How to calculate net salary from gross?

Net salary varies by country due to different social contribution rates.
Net ≈ Gross × (1 − contribution rate)

Gross salary is your total pay before deductions. Net salary is what you actually receive after social security contributions, national insurance, taxes at source, etc. The gap varies by country from ~13% (Switzerland) to ~27% (Belgium).

Each country funds different social programmes (healthcare, pension, unemployment) at different levels. Switzerland has a minimal welfare state → low deductions. Belgium has extensive coverage → higher deductions. The USA has lower mandatory contributions but fewer public benefits.

These are approximate estimates based on average employee contribution rates. Actual take-home pay depends on your specific contract, sector, benefits, income tax bracket, and local regulations. Always consult a payroll professional or official government calculator for precise figures.
