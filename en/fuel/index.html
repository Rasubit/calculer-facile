How to calculate fuel cost for a trip?

Formula: Cost = Distance × Consumption ÷ 100 × Price/L
or in MPG: Cost = Distance ÷ MPG × Price/gallon

Metric: distance (km) × consumption (L/100km) ÷ 100 × price ($/L). Imperial: distance (miles) ÷ MPG × price ($/gallon). Example: 300 miles, 30 MPG, $3.50/gal → 300 ÷ 30 × $3.50 = $35.

Fill up the tank, note the mileage, drive until next fill-up, note litres added and distance. Formula: litres ÷ km × 100 (or miles ÷ gallons for MPG).

MPG (US) = 235.21 ÷ L/100km. MPG (UK) = 282.48 ÷ L/100km. Example: 7 L/100km = 235.21 ÷ 7 = 33.6 MPG (US). Our calculator handles the conversion automatically.
